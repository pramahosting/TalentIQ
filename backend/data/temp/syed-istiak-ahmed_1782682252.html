    Most CEOs can read an income statement.
    Far fewer can read a cash flow statement with confidence.

    Credits to Oana Labes, MBA, CPA

    That’s the gap where the biggest financial blind spots live.

    📌 Want to learn more? Sign up for my free Cash Flow Masterclass:     https://bit.ly/49n7Lqh

    Here's my 10-step process for reading a cash flow statement:

    1️⃣ Start with Net Income
    ↳ The indirect method starts at the P&L, so that's where I start too.
    ↳ I look at the revenue and expenses driving that number to assess earnings quality.

    2️⃣ Non-Cash Adjustments
    ↳ Depreciation, amortization, gains and losses, stock-based compensation..
    ↳ Pay attention to understand what they were, even if they didn’t hit operating cash.

    3️⃣ Working Capital Changes
    ↳ I look at how receivables, inventory, and payables moved.
    ↳ Big swings here reveal the cost of growth or serious inefficiencies.

    4️⃣ Cash Flow from Operations
    ↳ When consistently positive it signals a business that sustains and grows itself.
    ↳ A persistent gap to Net Income can point to accounting manipulation or earnings quality concerns.

    5️⃣ Investing Activities
    ↳ High CapEx can mean future growth or future high maintenance costs. Context matters.
    ↳ Large outflows beyond CapEx reveals strategic plans or diversification moves.

    6️⃣ Financing Activities
    ↳ Debt issuance, repayments, equity raises, and distributions tell me how a company funds operations, investments, and returns value to shareholders.

    7️⃣ Net Cash Flow
    ↳ Simple but critical: did the cash position improve or deteriorate?

    8️⃣ Free Cash Flow
    ↳ CFO minus CapEx outflows. A partial(ly blind) measure of financial health

    9️⃣ Cash Flow Trends
    ↳ I never look at one period in isolation. Patterns across time reveal sustainability. Peer comparisons add context.

    🔟 Debt and Dividends
    ↳ Can FCF cover principal payments and dividend payouts? If not, everything else is noise.

    The bottom line:

    Reading a cash flow statement isn't just a finance task. It's a leadership responsibility.

    The leaders who own this skill don't react to cash problems.

    They anticipate them.

    Which side of that line are you on?f
