About

Do you spend a lot of time working on your business only to discover that you now have to worry about annoying paperwork or even worse, realising that you have missed an important lodgment deadline.

Wouldn’t it be nice to finish work at 5pm and head home to the family without having to worry about whether or not you have lodged your BAS on time, or maybe it’s that large tax bill you received unexpectedly last week and you don’t know where you will find the funds to pay it.

This is where I can help !

As a fully qualified Chartered Accountant with over 25 years experience in the industry, I spend my days helping business owners just like you. I own and operate a successful accounting practice that specialises in making sure clients accounting and tax obligations are prepared and lodged on-time, everytime. I also prepare strategies to reduce your tax and where possible, take advantage of any available government assistance packages.

My clients range from the single business owner trying to do everything on their own, right through to company directors managing a large team of staff and a multi million dollar business.

I love seeing business owners grow and thrive through every stage of business. So whether you are a startup business not knowing where to start or a mature business that has outgrown your current accountant’s capabilities then we would live to have a chat.

    Reminder for Employers: Super Guarantee Obligations

    The ATO is reminding employers to stay on top of their super guarantee (SG) responsibilities for all eligible employees.

    From 1 July 2025, the SG rate is 12% of an employee’s ordinary time earnings. These contributions must be paid quarterly into a complying super fund.
    👉 Next due date: 28 April 2026 for the March quarter.

    Key things employers need to know:
    🔹 Employees can usually choose their super fund
    🔹 If SG is not paid in full, on time, or to the correct fund, employers may face the SG charge, which includes:
    • the unpaid super
    • 10% nominal interest
    • $20 admin fee per employee, per quarter

    🔹 All payments must be made through SuperStream
    🔹 The Small Business Superannuation Clearing House will close permanently on 1 July 2026 — users should transition to another payment method soon

    New employees? One extra step may apply
    If a new employee doesn’t choose a fund, employers may need to request their stapled super fund details from the ATO to meet the choice‑of‑fund rules.

    Staying compliant avoids penalties and ensures your team’s retirement savings stay on track.

    If you need help reviewing your SG processes or transitioning away from the SBSCH, our team is here to support you.

    **🚨 Claiming Travel Allowances? Read This First! 🚨**

    The ATO is cracking down on travel allowance claims—even if you're under the "reasonable amount" threshold. That means **substantiation is still expected**. Here's what you need to know:

    ✅ **Diary Required**: Record every overnight trip—where you stayed, when you ate, and what you spent.
    ✅ **Sample Month of Receipts**: Keep receipts for meals and accommodation for at least one month (can be retrospective if your job hasn’t changed).
    ✅ **Employer Letter**: Must confirm number of nights away, locations, daily allowance breakdown, and that no expenses were reimbursed.
    ✅ **Bank Statements**: Card payments help prove personal expenditure—ditch the cash!

    💡 Pro Tip: If you're away for more than 6 nights, keep **all receipts** and a detailed diary. And yes—**audit insurance is highly recommended**.

    Let’s help you stay compliant and confident. Reach out if you need templates or support!

        📣 **Important Update for Small Business Owners!** 🧾💼

        The **Small Business Superannuation Clearing House (SBSCH)** will officially **close on 1 July 2026**. ⏳

        The SBSCH has been a free ATO service that lets employers pay super for all staff in one go — super handy! 💸 But now it’s time to prepare for the transition.

        🚫 **New registrations will close from 1 October 2025**
        ✅ **Existing users** should start exploring alternative options now

        💡 What can you do?

        🔍 Review your current payroll or accounting software — many already include super payment features
        🏦 Check with your super funds or commercial clearing houses for solutions
        🧑‍💻 Explore other payroll providers that offer integrated super services

        Don’t leave it to the last minute — get ahead and make the switch smoothly! 🛠️

        Need help navigating the change? We’re here to support you.
